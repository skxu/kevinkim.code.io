JINJU

SHORT (2014)
Yuri Lee is a second-generation Korean American. Her daughter, Jinju Pearl Lee, is having a particularly bad day and taking much of her frustration out on her mother. As we follow their story, we also see into Yuri's childhood with her own first-generation parents. This short film was sparked by interest in various ideas including the immigrant American experience. A key element of this narrative is the contrasting struggles each generation faces and what results from those differences. At its core, Jinju is a story about family, sacrifice, and forgiveness. We hurt the ones we love most, and we forgive because we love.
